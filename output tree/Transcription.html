Commentary:

Rectangular slab of marble rounded at the top and sides, broken at the upper left hand corner. It is darkened by a nail hole (by which the epitaph was originally attached to the wall of the columbaria (Wilson, 1914, 119)) and remaining nail on the middle right side, affecting the ends of lines 2 and 3. The face is affected by weathering, showing discoloration. The measurements are 19.3 X 28.5 X 3.6 cm. It is a funerary verse written in elegiac couplets to a female child (Soteris). There is one column and 6 lines, with even lines indented as a poetic convention to demonstrate the meter. While the text is well centered on the titulum, the spacing between lines is irregular (ranging from 2-1 cm) and there is notable space between the last line and the edge of the stone (4 cm, whereas only 2 cm from the top of the stone to the first line). The letters decrease in size down the rows of text.


Translation:

“Behold, under this inscription the bones of Soteris are located, having been consumed by merciless death, she lies in a tomb. She had not yet completed six years of life, when she was ordered to enter into the home of Black Dis. Those tears which the mother ought to surrender to her daughter, those same tears her daughter suddenly surrendered to her mother.”
